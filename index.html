Mechanical Engineering  ·  Ultrasonic NDE & Additive Manufacturing

I develop ultrasonic nondestructive evaluation methods for additively manufactured metals and complex heterogeneous media. My work integrates custom immersion ultrasound experiments with effective-medium theory and COMSOL simulations to understand and predict how waves propagate through process-induced microstructures.

Current research focuses on regolith–aluminum metal matrix composites for space applications, under an NSF Future Manufacturing grant in collaboration with NASA. I am a Bilsland Dissertation Fellow and the founding researcher of Purdue's IMPULSE Lab.
