Harshith Kumar
Adepu

Mechanical Engineering  ·  Ultrasonic NDE & Additive Manufacturing

I develop ultrasonic nondestructive evaluation methods for additively manufactured metals and complex media. My research integrates immersion ultrasound experiments with effective-medium theory and COMSOL modeling to guide NDE strategies for space materials, under an NSF Future Manufacturing grant in collaboration with NASA. I am a Bilsland Dissertation Fellow at Purdue's IMPULSE Lab.

What I Work On

Research

🔬
Ultrasonic NDE for AM Metals

Wave-speed, attenuation, and backscatter measurements on LPBF 316L stainless steel, establishing process-structure-property relationships via nondestructive evaluation.

🛸
Space Materials (NSF / NASA)

Modeling ultrasonic wave propagation in regolith–aluminum metal matrix composites using effective-medium theory and COMSOL, to enable NDE strategies for in-space manufacturing.

⚙️
Custom Immersion Ultrasound System

Designed and built a frugal laboratory-grade immersion ultrasonic test setup, now integrated into Purdue's ME496 NDE course and adopted as standard at the MMRL.

💧
Ultrasonic Sensing in Thermal Systems

Non-invasive ultrasonic detection of liquid-air interfaces and visualization of liquid-vapor dynamics in operating heat pipes, in collaboration with the Weibel Lab at Purdue.
