Research

NSF + NASA
Ultrasonic NDE for Space Materials

Modeling and measuring ultrasonic wave propagation in regolith–aluminum metal matrix composites (MMC) to develop NDE strategies for in-space and extraterrestrial additive manufacturing. Integrates effective-medium theory with COMSOL simulations and immersion measurements across a range of particulate volume fractions.

Process–Structure–Property via Ultrasound

Systematic study of how laser powder bed fusion process parameters (laser power, scan speed, hatch spacing) determine microstructure and how those microstructures manifest in ultrasonic wave speed, attenuation, and backscatter. Establishes a nondestructive route to the process–structure–property triangle.

Frugal Ultrasonic Immersion Test System

Designed and built a low-cost, laboratory-grade immersion ultrasound system from the ground up. Documented as an open methodology now integrated into Purdue's ME496 NDE course (60+ students) and adopted as standard instrumentation at the Purdue MMRL. Published in JASA Express Letters, 2025.

Ultrasonic Sensing in Heat Pipes

Non-invasive detection and visualization of liquid-air and liquid-vapor interfaces in enclosed volumes and operating heat pipes using pulse-echo ultrasound. Enables real-time, in-situ monitoring of two-phase flow without optical access. Collaboration with the Weibel Lab at Purdue.
